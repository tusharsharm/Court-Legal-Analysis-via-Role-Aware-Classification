The petition under Article 226 was affirmed on behalf of the appellant by an affidavit of Mohammad Khaliloor Rahaman sworn on the 11th March, 1965.	Fact

In the petition, five several notices dated the 4th December, 1964, under section 148 of the I.T. Act, 1961, for the assessment years 1955-56 to 1959-60 were challenged.	Fact
It appears that the appellants income for the assessment years 1953-54 and 1955-56 were assessed on an agreed basis at a flat rate of 9 per cent, on the total turnover of each year.	Fact
The appellant is a registered partnership firm and carries on business, inter alia, in mining and export of manganese, chrome and other kinds of mineral ores.	Fact

The revenue authorities made enquiries and investigations with a view to a settlement for the assessment years 1952-53 to 1959-60.	Fact

It is alleged that the I.T. authorities found certain discrepancies in various assessments.	Fact
On the 18th January, 1961, the ITO wrote to the appellant stating that as many as eleven categories of discrepancies in connection with pending assessment proceedings as well as the settlement petition had been found.	Fact
The discrepancy Number VIII which the ITO had referred to related to under-invoicing amounting to Rs. 29 lakhs.	Fact
The appellant gave its reply to the ITOs letter on February 25, 1961.	Fact

The tax authorities made investigations based on the allegations in the letter of the 18th January, 1961.	Fact
On the 27th March, 1961, a settlement was arrived at between the petitioner and its associate companies on the one hand and the CIT on the other, whereby the assessments and penalties of the petitioner were settled for the assessment years 1952-53 to 1959-60.	Fact

Fact

Let us go through some of the relevant provisions of the 1961 Act directly applicable to this appeal.	None
In Section 147(a) it is stated, inter alia, that if the ITO has reason to believe that, by reason of the omission or failure on the part of an assessee to disclose fully and truly all material facts necessary for his assessment for any assessment year, income chargeable to tax has escaped assessment for that year, he may, subject to the provisions, inter alia, of Section 148(2) and Section 151 assess or reassess such income.	Statute
Under this provision, therefore, the ITO has the power to reassess income if he has reason to believe that, by reason of the omission or failure on the part of an assessee to disglose fully and truly all material facts necessary for his assessment for any assessment year, income chargeable to tax for that year has escaped assessment.	Statute
But to take action under section 147(a) the ITO is subjected to various restrictions.	Statute
Sub-section (2) of Section 148 requires that the ITO shall, before issuing any notice under section 148, record his reasons for doing so.	Statute
There are further restrictions in Section 151.	Statute
Sub-section (1) of Section 151 provides that no notice shall be issued under section 148 after the expiry of eight years from the end of the relevant assessment year, unless the Board is satisfied, on the reasons recorded by the ITO, that it is a fit case for the issue of such notice.	Statute
Sub-section (2) of Section 151 prescribes that no notice shall be issued under section 148 after the expiry of four years from the end of the relevant assessment year, unless the Commissioner is satisfied, on the reasons recorded by the ITO, that it is a fit case for the issue of such notice.	Statute
In the instant case, for the assessment year 1955-56, the sanction has been given by the Board.	Fact
For the assessment years 1956-57 to 1959-60, the sanction has been given by the Commissioner.	Fact

7-9-64 Learned counsel for the appellant has urged before us that in the investigation report which has been shown to us, the ITO has merely made a proposal to reopen the assessments for the assessment years in question.	ArgumentPetitioner
There is no indication at all in this report of the formation of any belief that due to the failure of the assessee to disclose fully and truly all material facts necessary for the assessment for the relevant years, the income chargeable to tax has escaped assessment.	ArgumentPetitioner
On the basis of such an investigation report, therefore, neither the Board nor the Commissioner should have given sanction to reopen the assessments and as such all the purported notices under section 148 dated the 4th December, 1964, should be struck down.	ArgumentPetitioner
Now, Sub-section (2) of Section 148 requires that before issuing any notice under section 148 the ITO must record his reasons for issuing the notice.	RatioOfTheDecision
This subsection does not also require that he should state that he has formed the belief that, by reason of the omission or failure of the assessee, income has escaped assessment.	RatioOfTheDecision
This belief of his can be inferred from the reasons recorded and the manner in which the recording has been made.	RatioOfTheDecision
Each case has to be determined on its own facts.	RatioOfTheDecision
This is a case in which at no point of time the assessee has produced on its own any books, papers or documents relating to the assessees income.	RatioOfTheDecision
Some of these books, papers and documents were seen by the revenue authorities before the settlement was arrived at in 1961.	RatioOfTheDecision
It is apparent from the report of the 7th September, 1964, that after the settlement the revenue authorities have examined other books, papers and documents which had not been either discovered or examined at all before the settlement and from the examination of these other books, papers and documents, the ITO has reached the conclusion in his report that the assessee had adopted cunning and ingenious devices in its operational activities which resulted in escapement of assessment.	RatioOfTheDecision
Upon consideration of this investigation report of the 7th September, 1964, we are clearly of opinion that in the instant case all the requirements of Sections 147(a), 148 and 151 have been complied with before the issue of the notices under section 148 dated the 4th December, 1964.	RatioOfTheDecision
Had this report been placed earlier before us, we would not have spent such a long time on this appeal.	RatioOfTheDecision
The recorded reasons, in our opinion, establish sufficiently that there was failure on the part of the assessee to disclose fully and truly all material facts relating to the relevant assessments.	RatioOfTheDecision
It is clear that many of the under-invoicings concerning transactions in chrome and manganese ores were not known to the I.T. authorities when they made the settlement in 1961.	RatioOfTheDecision
We are, therefore, inclined to agree with the conclusions which the learned trial judge has reached.	RatioOfTheDecision
