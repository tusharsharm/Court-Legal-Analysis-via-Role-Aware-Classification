Order under Section 26(2) of the Competition Act, 2002 The information bearing Case Number 05 of 2016 has been filed under Section 19(1) (a) of the Competition Act, 2002 (hereinafter, the Act) by Mr. Mukul Kumar Govil Mrs. Kiran Govil against ET Infra Developers Pvt.	Fact
Limited (hereinafter, OP 1) and Noida Development Authority (hereinafter, OP 2) alleging, inter alia, contravention of the provisions of Sections 3 4 of the Act.	Fact
Another information bearing Case Number 06 of 2016 has been filed under Section 19(1)(a) of the Act by Mrs. Anshoo Bansal Mr. Amit Bansal against OP 1 and OP 2 alleging, inter alia, contravention of the provisions of Sections 3 4 of the Act.	Fact
The Commission considered both the information and found that though these information have been filed separately by different Informants, they are against the same opposite parties, relate to same project and bring out similar allegations.	Fact
As the facts allegation raised therein are substantially similar, the Commission is satisfied that these can be clubbed together.	Fact
Therefore, this common order shall dispose of both the information.	Fact
OP 1 is stated to be a private limited company, engaged in the development of residential and commercial space.	Fact
It has been stated that both the Informants had booked single office unit each in the World Trade Tower - Business Avenue (WTT) project developed by OP 1 at Noida.	Fact
In this regard, OP 1 has also issued allotment letters to both the Informants.	Fact
Both the Informants have identified OP 2 as Noida Development Authority but as such no such authority exist.	Fact
It appears that both the Informants are referring to New Okhla Industrial Development Authority (OP 2).	Fact
OP 2 is constituted under the U.P. Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, inter alia, to acquire land in the notified area through Govt.	Fact
of UP under Land Acquisition Act, 1894 or by way of agreement to prepare Master Plan for development of the area to demarcate and develop sites for various land uses to allot plot properties as per regulations to regulate the erection of buildings and setting up industries and to provide infrastructure and amenities.	Fact
Both the Informants have alleged that OP 1, in collusion with OP 2, to avoid pressure from allottees, had issued an illegal exorbitant demand notice for a certain amount to be deposited but had not handed over the possession within the stipulated period as committed in the allotment letters.	ArgumentPetitioner
As per clause 19 of the allotment letter, OP 1 had to deliver the fully completed office space within 36 months from the date of allotment letter.	ArgumentPetitioner
However, allegedly OP 1 has not offered possession even after the expiry of more than 5 years.	ArgumentPetitioner
In terms of clause 21 of the allotment letter, OP 1 is liable to pay compensation at the rate of Rs. 40/- per sq.	ArgumentPetitioner
ft. per month for the period of delay in possession.	ArgumentPetitioner
Both the Informants have alleged that OP 1 has failed to pay the said compensation and interest thereon at the rate of 18 p.a. for delay in payment of compensation.	ArgumentPetitioner
Further, as a result of delay in handing over possession, both the Informants could not operate their businesses through the said offices which according to them has also resulted in loss of goodwill.	ArgumentPetitioner
Both the Informants have further alleged that OP 1 vide demand notices dated 19th September, 2015 had intimated them to pay the remaining amount, actually due only at the time of possession.	ArgumentPetitioner
However, allegedly the said office units are not yet ready for possession as flooring is incomplete, electric wiring and water connection are yet to be done and public utility is not in existence.	ArgumentPetitioner
It has also been averred that no objection certificate (NOC) and permissions have also not been obtained by OP 1 from various government agencies.	ArgumentPetitioner
It has been alleged that OP 1 had also issued demand letters on 28th November, 2015 requiring both the Informants to pay various exorbitant and illegal charges such as one time lease rent, annual lease rent for period prior to the execution of lease deed, LEED Pre-certified Gold Rated Green Building Charges and electrification charges.	ArgumentPetitioner
Both the Informants have also stated that since OP 1 has already received around 95 of sale consideration for the said office units from them, their purchasing power has been curtailed and as a result, they have been denied market access to purchase other office units.	ArgumentPetitioner
With regard to dominance, both the Informants have stated that OP 1 is the only developer for whom OP 2 has allotted land for development of commercial project in a prime location.	ArgumentPetitioner
Hence, OP 1 has been alleged to enjoy a dominant position.	ArgumentPetitioner
Both the Informants have prayed to the Commission, inter alia, to restrain OP 1 from demanding various unfair and exorbitant charges to direct OP 1 to pay compensation for delay in possession and initiate enquiry under Section 26(1) of the Act.	ArgumentPetitioner
The Commission has given a careful consideration to the material available on record.	Fact
Since the allegations pertain to violation of the provisions of Section 4 of the Act, the relevant market in terms of Section 2(r) of the Act is required to be delineated before examining the alleged abusive conduct of OP 1.	RatioOfTheDecision
The Commission notes that the matter relates to sale of commercial office units in a project developed by OP 1.	RatioOfTheDecision
The real estate market can be broadly classified into two main segment: residential and commercial.	RatioOfTheDecision
Residential segment can be further sub-categorized into residential apartment flat and plots.	RatioOfTheDecision
The Commission observes that sale of commercial units form a separate relevant product market in terms of the provisions of the Act, because the intention and factors considered while buying a commercial office unit are different from buying a residential flat or plot.	RatioOfTheDecision
Further, the requirements and prospects of a consumer buying a commercial office unit are also different from those of a consumer buying a residential flat or plot.	RatioOfTheDecision
Thus, taking into account factors such as substitutability, characteristics of service offered, price and intended use provision of services for development and sale of commercial space appears to be the relevant product market in the present case.	RatioOfTheDecision
With regard to the relevant geographic market, the Commission is of the view that the consumers, looking for a commercial office unit in Noida Greater Noida, may not prefer other areas.	RatioOfTheDecision
Various factors like availability of office space, location of business establishment, transport connectivity etc.	RatioOfTheDecision
play a decisive role in a potential buyers decision making process while choosing a commercial office unit in a particular area.	RatioOfTheDecision
Further, the geographic region of Noida Greater Noida exhibits homogenous and distinct market conditions as compared to other regions of NCR such as Faridabad, Delhi and Gurgaon etc.	RatioOfTheDecision
Considering these aspects, the Commission opines that the relevant geographic market in the instant case would be Noida Greater Noida.	RatioOfTheDecision
Thus, the relevant market would be the market of provision of services for development and sale of commercial space in Noida Greater Noida.	RatioOfTheDecision
As regards dominance, the Commission notes that the underlying principle for assessing dominance of an enterprise is linked to the market power enjoyed by the enterprise.	RatioOfTheDecision
An enterprise could be regarded as dominant if it enjoys possesses a position of strength in the relevant market, which enables it to operate independently of competitive forces prevailing in the relevant market or affect its competitors or consumers or the relevant market in its favour.	RatioOfTheDecision
It is observed that no data has been provided by both the Informants in support of their assertion of dominance of OP 1 in the relevant market.	RatioOfTheDecision
The Commission, however, observes that other than OP 1, there are many other real estate developers such as Jaypee Green, Omaxe, Supertech Limited, AIRWIL, Wave City Center, Lotus Greens and Saviour Builders Pvt.	RatioOfTheDecision
Limited etc.	RatioOfTheDecision
, rendering similar services in the relevant market.	RatioOfTheDecision
The competitors of OP 1 are found to have projects of varying magnitude and are comparable with OP 1 in terms of size and resources.	RatioOfTheDecision
The presence of other players indicates that the buyers have various options.	RatioOfTheDecision
Accordingly, OP 1 does not appear to enjoy a dominant position in the relevant market.	RatioOfTheDecision
In the absence of dominance, the examination of alleged abusive conduct does not arise.	RatioOfTheDecision
Therefore, the Commission is of the view that there is no contravention of provisions of Section 4 of the Act in the instant matter.	RatioOfTheDecision
The Commission observes that both the Informants have also alleged collusion between OP 1 and OP 2.	RatioOfTheDecision
It has been stated that OP 1 is the only developer for whom OP 2 has allotted land for such commercial project in prime location.	RatioOfTheDecision
The Commission observes that other builder have also been allotted lands for development of commercial space as discussed supra and there does not exist any information as such to support the contention that there exists an agreement between OP 1 and OP 2 which is in violation of the provisions of Section 3 of the Act.	RatioOfTheDecision
In view of above, no case of contravention is made out against the opposite parties under Sections 3 or 4 of the Act.	RatioOfTheDecision
Accordingly, these cases are hereby directed to be closed under Section 26(2) of the Act.	RulingByPresentCourt
The Secretary is directed to inform all concerned accordingly.	RulingByPresentCourt
