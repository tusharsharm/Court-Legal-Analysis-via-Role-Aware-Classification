On 30.07.2015, the Competition Commission of India (Commission) received a notice under sub-section (2) of Section 6 of the Competition Act, 2002 (Act) given by Baxalta (Notice).	Fact
The Notice has been filed pursuant to the execution of Global Separation and Distribution Agreement (GSDA) between Baxter International Inc. (Baxter) and Baxalta, on 30.06.2015.	Fact
As per the details provided in the Notice, the combination relates to the acquisition by Baxalta of the bioscience business and related assets of Baxter (Target Business).	Fact
Baxter and Baxalta are stated to be public limited companies registered in USA and listed on the New York Stock Exchange.	Fact
As per the information given in the Notice, Baxter provides a broad portfolio of bioscience products (constituting the Target Business), intravenous solutions and nutritional therapies, drug delivery systems, etc.	Fact
As stated in the Notice, Baxalta, prior to the combination, was a wholly owned subsidiary of Baxter and did not carry out any business activities.	Fact
It has been submitted in the Notice that pursuant to the GSDA, the Target Business has been effectively transferred to the Acquirer on 30.06.2015 (Global Implementation), except in certain deferred jurisdictions, including India, where the transfer of the local target businesses will take place at a later date pursuant to local separation agreements (hereinafter, the implementation of the combination in India is referred to as the India Separation).	Fact
Further, on 01.07.2015, Baxter distributed shares of Baxalta on a pro rata basis to its shareholders, cumulatively amounting to 80.50 per cent of Baxaltas shareholding and the remaining 19.50 per cent equity shares were retained by Baxter itself.	Fact
In relation to business operations of Baxter in India, it has been submitted that it operates in India through two subsidiaries, namely, Baxter India Private Limited and Gambro India Private Limited.	Fact
Under the India Separation, Baxters Indian bioscience business and related assets will be transferred to a newly created wholly owned subsidiary of Baxter, viz. Baxalta Bio Science (India) Private Limited (Baxalta India).	Fact
Thereafter, the ownership and control of Baxalta India will be transferred to Baxalta.	Fact
As already stated, prior to the Global Implementation, Baxalta was a wholly owned subsidiary of the Baxter, and did not carry out any business activities.	RatioOfTheDecision
Thus, there was no horizontal overlap or vertical relationship between the business activities of Baxter and Baxalta.	RatioOfTheDecision
It is also observed that the combination, including the eventual India Separation, relates to a structural separation of the Target Business from Baxter into a newly incorporated company, Baxalta.	RatioOfTheDecision
Accordingly, the said structural change is unlikely to result in any impact on competition in any market(s) in India.	RatioOfTheDecision
Considering the facts on record and the details provided in the notice given under sub-section (2) of Section 6 of the Act and the assessment of the combination after considering the relevant factors mentioned in sub-section (4) of Section 20 of the Act, the Commission is of the opinion that the combination is not likely to have any appreciable adverse effect on competition in India and therefore, the Commission hereby approves the combination under sub-section (1) of Section 31 of the Act.	RulingByPresentCourt
This order is issued without prejudice to any proceedings under Section 43A of the Act.	RulingByPresentCourt
This order shall stand revoked if, at any time, the information provided by the Acquirer is found to be incorrect.	RulingByPresentCourt
The Secretary is directed to communicate to the Acquirer accordingly.	RulingByPresentCourt
