The material facts which are absolutely essential to determine the controversy, eschewing the unnecessary details, may now be recapitulated: Factual Background: With the decision of the Government of India, more than 25 years ago, ushering into era of globalisation and liberalisation, lot of avenues opened up.	Fact
It led to the privatisation of business in many sectors which were, hitherto, monopolistic domain of the Government.	Fact
These included aviation, insurance, telecommunication etc.	Fact
With the opening of the industrial and other activities in all spheres by placing it in the hands of private sector led to a significant economic development.	Fact
The absolute control of the Government through public enterprise or otherwise, which had seen licence and quota raj, virtually withered away, thereby reverting back to laissez faire economy to a great extent, though not completely.	Fact
It led to two significant developments: In the first instance, though the private sector was given full freedom to do the business without any shackles in the form of controls etc., it was also deemed necessary at the same time that in public interest, some of the aspects of the business need to be regulated, of course, not by the Government but by an independent regulatory authority.	Fact
This necessity prompted the Government to come out with regulatory regime in different sectors.	Fact
For example, in insurance sector, we have regulatory authority constituted under Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority Act, 1999 for industries generating electricity, there is an electricity regulatory authority constituted under the Electricity Act, 2003 and for telecom sector, with which we are concerned, the TRAI is constituted under the provisions of TRAI Act.	Fact
Secondly, this requirement to do business thereby allowing free entry to private enterprise led to competition between different players in the private sector.	Fact
Competition is perceived as a phenomena which is in best public interest in so many ways.	Fact
Therefore, it becomes necessary to encourage competition.	Fact
At the same time, tendency of the business enterprises to adopt practices which retard healthy competition needed to be curbed.	Fact
There was a governing law in the field known as Monopolistic and Restrictive Trade Practice Act, 1969.	Fact
However, it was felt that a new robust statutory regime is required to take care of the needs of the present day.	Fact
This necessity prompted the Parliament to come out with a new Act on the subject and the Competition Act, 2002 was passed by the Parliament.	Fact
Under this Act, the CCI is constituted as a statutory body which is to ensure healthy competition in markets thereby preventing the practice of having adverse effect on competition to promote and sustain the competition in markets to protect the interest of consumers and to ensure freedom of trade.	Fact
In that sense, the CCI is also a regulator.	Fact
But a unique feature of the CCI is that it is not sector based body but has the jurisdiction across which transcends sectoral boundaries, thereby covering all the industries, with focus on the aforesaid object and purpose behind the Competition Act, 2002.	Fact
In the instant appeals, width and scope of the powers of the CCI under the Competition Act, 2002 pertaining to telecom sector i.e. in respect of the companies in telecom industry providing telecom services is to be defined vis-a-vis the scope of the powers of TRAI under the TRAI Act, 1997.	Fact
It has arisen in these appeals, in the following background: As mentioned above, TRAI is the regulatory which regulates the functioning of the telecom service provider i.e. the telecom sector.	Fact
Section 11 of the TRAI Act enumerates various functions which TRAI is supposed to perform under the Act.	Fact
Section 13, likewise, empowers the TRAI to issue directions, from time to time, to the service provider.	Fact
In exercise of powers Under Section 13 read with Section 11 of the TRAI Act, the TRAI issued directions dated June 07, 2005 to all the telecom service providers to provide interconnection within ninety days of the applicable payments made by the interconnection seeker.	Fact
The purpose behind providing interconnection by one service provider to the other service provider is to ensure smooth communication by a subscriber of one service provider to the cell number which is provided by another service provider.	Fact
In that sense, this direction facilitates smooth functioning of the cell phone network even when it is managed by different companies as it ensures interconnectivity i.e. connectivity from one service provider to other service provider.	Fact

Stated simply, the fact or facts upon which the jurisdiction of a court, a tribunal or an authority depends can be said to be a jurisdictional fact.	PrecedentReliedUpon
If the jurisdictional fact exists, a court, tribunal or authority has jurisdiction to decide other issues.	PrecedentReliedUpon
If such fact does not exist, a court, tribunal or authority cannot act.	PrecedentReliedUpon
It is also well settled that a court or a tribunal cannot wrongly assume existence of jurisdictional fact and proceed to decide a matter.	PrecedentReliedUpon
The underlying principle is that by erroneously assuming existence of a jurisdictional fact, a subordinate court or an inferior tribunal cannot confer upon itself jurisdiction which it otherwise does not posses.	PrecedentReliedUpon
